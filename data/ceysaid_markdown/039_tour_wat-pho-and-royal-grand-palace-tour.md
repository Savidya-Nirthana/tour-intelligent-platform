Discover the magic of Bangkok, a city that seamlessly blends the ancient with the modern. Explore the awe-inspiring temples and palaces, from the towering spire of Wat Arun to the grandeur of the Grand Palace and the peaceful tranquility of Wat Pho. Marvel at the largest solid gold statue in the world, the Golden Buddha, and be enchanted by the Emerald Buddha, a highly revered statue carved from a single block of jade. **Itinerary**

* 08:30 AM: Pick up from the various hotels.
* 09:00 AM: The tourists will be transferred to Wat Trimit, the temple of the golden Buddha with 5.5 tonnes of solid gold, sculptured in Sukhothai style.
* 10:00 AM: Drive through Chinatown, Indian Town, Flower Market, and the Central business district of Bangkok.
* 10:30 AM: Visit Wat Pho, the Temple of the reclining Buddha, a stunning 46 m long and 15 m high covered in gold leaf.
* 11.30 AM: Visit the Royal Grand Palace & Emerald Buddha: This city landmark should be the first place on any visitor’s itinerary, which features some of the most spectacular architecture in Southeast Asia. The palace began in 1782. It is a consisting of several buildings with highly decorated architectural designs.
* Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of the Emerald Buddha) The magnificent Royal Chapel, Wat Phra Kaew in the same compound enshrines the Emerald Buddha Image, most revered by people.
* 01:00 PM: Lunch at a Local Restaurant
* 02:00 PM: Cross Chao Phraya River by boat to visit Wat Arun. (Temple of Dawn) The most attractive in this temple is the pagoda Khmer - style. Wat Arun was restored during the brief Thonburi period to be the Royal Chapel of King Taksin.
